FAQ & Resource Center

Quick answers to common questions about our structure, pricing, and services.

Service Scope and Credentials

Are you a CPA firm or a Law Firm?
No. Hennig Financial LLC is neither a CPA firm nor a law firm. We specialize in tax preparation, bookkeeping, and consulting services. We do not provide legal advice or financial statement audit/review services.
What authority do you have to deal with the IRS?
The owner holds an IRS Annual Filing Season Program (AFSP) Record of Completion. This grants **limited** representation rights before the IRS for returns prepared and signed by this firm. For audits, collections, or appeals related to those returns, we can often assist. For complex litigation or tax court matters, clients must seek an Enrolled Agent (EA) or Tax Attorney.
What types of businesses are NOT a fit for your services?
We specialize in simplified services for individuals and small businesses. We **cannot** accept clients who require audited financial statements, have annual revenues over $5 million, are publicly traded, or have complex international tax structures. Please see the Service Scope on the Pricing tab for a full list of exclusions.

Pricing and Discounts

Why do some services cost more if my records are messy?
Pricing for items like Schedule C preparation increases if we have to spend multiple hours organizing bank statements, categorizing transactions, or reconciling funds. Our interactive calculator prices services based on **clean, summarized data**, which is why cleanup fees are often required upfront.
How can I find out if I qualify for a discount?
We offer limited promotional discounts for early clients, specific community members, and local businesses in the New Jersey area. These discounts are applied via a unique access code on the quote estimator. Please feel free to reach out to Ron or Mikaela directly to see if any discount codes are currently available for your situation.

HR and Payroll Compliance

Is your HR Advisory Retainer legal advice?
No. Our HR support is for administrative guidance, process best practices, and compliance questions only. For employment law matters, complex legal contracts, or litigation support, we strongly recommend consulting a licensed attorney.
